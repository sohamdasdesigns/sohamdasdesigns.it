the_other_code



The words that come alive on a blank page while the writer speaks from his imagination, his thoughts, ideas, feelings, to communicate with others, with the future or maybe even himself.

In a practical sense, the code is an instruction manual for a machine, but in a much broader sense, it's a mode of communication for the programmer with his tool, and later with the world. The same way a painter sharpens a pencil or lays colours on a palette, the writer turns a new blank page, a programmer puts a new *untitled document in his screen but the goal remains the same, “to express” the ideas, the thoughts, the feelings. That may be a title of a book written in elegant calligraphy, a brush stroke of ultramarine blue on a snow white canvas or a piece of algorithm written on a code editor to generate a few aesthetic pixels on a screen.

If the curiosity of human minds remains inspired towards finding new ways to see the world, or maybe think about the world in a different way, to explore its own boundaries, then, art remains meaningful with its essence and all its different ways too.
